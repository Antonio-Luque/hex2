6100: Cyan Boards
Initialized by the routine at PathFinder_CopyPositions. Read by the routines at PathFinder_RedScore, PathFinder_CyanScore, Candidates_Winner, Candidates_Defeat, PathFinder_Candidates, PathFinder_Potentials, PathFinder_Cyan_UpDown, PathFinder_Cyan_DownUp, Path_Finder, Path_Finder_Adjnt, PathFinder_CYAN_Edge_0, PathFinder_CYAN_Edge_1, PathFinder_CYAN_Edge_2, PathFinder_CYAN_Edge_3, PathFinder_CYAN_Edge_4, PathFinder_CYAN_Edge_5, PathFinder_CYAN_Edge_6 and PathFinder_VictoryPath. Updated by the routines at PathFinder_Update and PathFinder_PrepareBoard.
These boards hold a determined position of the board from cyan perspective. They are used to calculate CYAN "two- distance" (Jack van Rijswijck's thesis) from UP to DOWN edges and CYAN two-distance from DOWN to UP edges. The cyan UP-DOWN board is also used to store CYAN potentials and TOTAL potentials.
The values of $7F form the border of the board. The UP and DOWN edges are identified with 7th bit set and the lowest 3 bits are the edge number. The rest of bytes indicates "two-distance" from goal (stored in lowest 6 bits), and a flag that indicates a position occupied by a friendly token (stored in 6th bit).
